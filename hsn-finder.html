What is HSN Code?

HSN (Harmonized System of Nomenclature) is an international system of names and numbers for classifying goods. In India, it's used to determine which GST rate applies to a product. Every product has a unique HSN code.

Who Needs to Use HSN Code?

Goods Traders (Turnover > ₹5 Crore): Compulsory to mention HSN codes on invoices.

Service Providers (Turnover > ₹20 Lakh): Must use SAC (Service Accounting Code) instead of HSN.

Others: Optional but recommended for clarity and GST compliance.

Understanding HSN Structure

HSN codes can be 2, 4, 6, or 8 digits:

  • 2 digits: Chapter (broad category)
  • 4 digits: Heading (sub-category)
  • 6 digits: Sub-heading (more specific)
  • 8 digits: Tariff item (most specific)

For example, cotton fabrics fall under Chapter 52 (textiles), heading 5208 (cotton fabrics), etc.

How to Find the Correct HSN Code?

1. Search in the list above using the product name or any partial code.

2. Check the official CBIC (Central Board of Indirect Taxes and Customs) website for comprehensive HSN listings.

3. Contact a CA or tax professional for complex products.

4. If unsure, use the most general HSN code that applies to your product category.

Note:

The HSN codes shown above cover the 200 most common products and services. For rare or specialized items, please check the official CBIC website or consult a tax professional.
