Understanding CGST, SGST, and IGST

CGST (Central GST): This is the GST collected by the Central Government. In an intra-state transaction (both parties in the same state), CGST is applied at half of the total GST rate. For example, on an 18% GST rate, CGST is 9%.

SGST (State GST): This is the GST collected by the State Government. Like CGST, in an intra-state transaction, SGST is also applied at half of the total GST rate. So CGST + SGST = total GST.

IGST (Integrated GST): In an inter-state transaction (where the seller and buyer are in different states), the full GST is collected as IGST by the Central Government. No CGST or SGST is levied.

Example: If you buy goods worth ₹1,000 from your state at 18% GST: You pay CGST ₹90 + SGST ₹90 = ₹180 total tax. But if you buy from a different state, you pay IGST ₹180 instead (same amount, but it all goes to central government).
