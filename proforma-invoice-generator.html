What is a Proforma Invoice?

A proforma invoice is a preliminary bill of sale sent to a buyer prior to the shipment of goods or delivery of services. It details the estimated costs, product descriptions, terms of supply, and shipping logistics. Unlike a standard commercial tax invoice, a proforma invoice is a commitment to supply rather than a formal request for payment or a legal tax record. It establishes mutual trust, acts as a firm quote, and enables clients to arrange internal purchase approvals or import clearances.

Using a proforma invoice generator allows businesses to issue structured, professional quote sheets. In import-export trade or large corporate service projects, a client rarely pays without a formal document detailing exactly what is to be delivered. The proforma document functions as that initial draft, allowing the customer to review the transaction layout, agree on pricing, and make advance payments.

Proforma Invoice vs. Commercial Invoice vs. Quotation

Understanding the distinction between these three documents is essential for correct bookkeeping:

  • Quotation: An informal proposal outlining estimated rates, subject to change and negotiation. It does not bind either party.
  • Proforma Invoice: A binding agreement showing the exact terms of sale. It is issued before the work begins or goods are shipped, often to secure advance payment. It does not record actual accounts receivable or act as a tax claim.
  • Commercial/Tax Invoice: The final legal document issued after supply has occurred. It records the final transaction value, calculates legal taxes (like GST), and acts as the official tax record for input credit claims.

When Should Your Business Issue a Proforma Invoice?

Incorporate proforma documents into your billing pipeline in these standard business scenarios:

  • Securing Advance Payments: If you require partial or full payments before manufacturing goods or beginning a consulting project.
  • Custom Clearance & Import Licences: In international trade, customs departments require a proforma invoice to assess import duties and clear cargo.
  • Internal Corporate Approvals: Corporate accounts departments require a proforma invoice to issue a purchase order (PO) and approve budget allocations.
  • Project Milestone Billing: For long-term contracting or software development, proforma invoices confirm the scope and value of milestones before final billing.

GST Rules for Proforma Invoicing in India

Under India's GST regulations, a proforma invoice is not a legal tax document and does not allow buyers to claim Input Tax Credit (ITC). It does not require registration on government e-invoice portals. However, to maintain clean auditing, the document should match the layout of the final tax invoice, listing correct HSN codes, buyer GSTINs, and estimated tax calculations. Once the advance is received or supply occurs, you must issue a final GST tax invoice to comply with the tax department.
