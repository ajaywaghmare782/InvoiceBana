What is a GST Invoice?

Published on January 2025 • 8 min read

Introduction

A GST Invoice is a document issued by a registered business to its buyer that contains details of goods or services supplied along with the amount of GST charged. It's one of the most important documents in Indian business as it serves as proof of the transaction and is required for GST compliance.

Definition of GST Invoice

According to Section 2(75) of the CGST Act, 2017, a GST Invoice is an invoice that contains all the mandatory information prescribed by GST Rule 46 and issued by a registered taxable person to a recipient.

Who Needs to Issue GST Invoices?

  • Registered Businesses: Any business registered under GST must issue GST invoices to their buyers
  • Inter-State Supplies: Even if intra-state supplies don't require invoices in some cases, inter-state supplies always need them
  • B2B Transactions: Business-to-business transactions always require invoices
  • E-commerce Transactions: Online sellers must issue invoices
  • Services: Service providers, consultants, and freelancers (if registered) must issue invoices

Mandatory Fields in a GST Invoice

According to GST Rule 46, a GST invoice must contain the following information:

1. Invoice Details

  • Serial number of the invoice
  • Date of issue of the invoice
  • Type of invoice (Tax Invoice, Bill of Supply, Proforma Invoice, etc.)

2. Seller Information

  • Business name and address
  • GSTIN (GST Registration Number) of the supplier
  • State and State Code

3. Buyer Information

  • Buyer's name and address
  • GSTIN (if the buyer is registered)
  • State and State Code
  • E-way bill number (if applicable and required)

4. Item Details

  • Description of goods/services
  • HSN (Harmonized System of Nomenclature) for goods
  • SAC (Service Accounting Code) for services
  • Quantity and unit of measurement
  • Unit price
  • Applicable discount or rebate

5. Tax Details

  • Tax rate (0%, 5%, 12%, 18%, or 28%)
  • SGST amount (State GST)
  • CGST amount (Central GST)
  • IGST amount (Integrated GST) — for inter-state supplies
  • CESS (if applicable)

6. Totals

  • Total taxable value
  • Total tax amount
  • Grand total (in words and figures)

7. Additional Information

  • Seller's name, title, and signature (for printed invoices) or digital signature
  • Recipient's name and signature (optional but recommended)
  • Payment terms
  • Notes and terms

Types of GST Invoices

1. Tax Invoice

Issued for supply of taxable goods or services where GST is charged. This is the most common type.

2. Bill of Supply

Issued for supply of exempt goods or services or for supplies made by unregistered businesses.

3. Proforma Invoice

Not a legal invoice but a quotation issued before actual supply. Used to show expected price and terms.

4. Credit Note

Issued when goods are returned or services are canceled after the original invoice.

5. Debit Note

Issued for increase in price due to error or additional supply of goods/services.

GST Invoice Format

While GST law specifies what information must be included, the format and layout are flexible. You can include more information if needed, but all mandatory fields must be present. Many businesses use:

  • Traditional printed invoices
  • Digital/PDF invoices
  • Online invoicing software
  • Accounting packages (like Tally, QuickBooks)
  • Free tools like InvoiceBana

Digital Signatures and E-Invoicing

For invoices above ₹50 lakh in value, digital signatures are mandatory (as per recent amendments). For e-invoicing, invoices must be registered on the IRP (Invoice Registration Portal) operated by GSTN (Goods and Services Tax Network).

Important Points to Remember

  • Invoices must be issued within 30 days of supply (or as per different timelines for different types of supplies)
  • Invoice number must be unique and sequential
  • Invoice must be in English or any other language specified by the state
  • Invoices must be retained for at least 6 years
  • Original invoices must be issued to the buyer; copies are for your records
  • Invoices cannot be altered after issuance (except through credit/debit notes)
