Mandatory GST Invoice Fields: Complete Compliance Checklist

1. Why Invoicing Details Matter under GST

Under the Goods and Services Tax (GST) laws in India, a tax invoice is the primary record for tax collection and Input Tax Credit (ITC) verification. For tax authorities, it validates that tax has been collected by the seller and must be deposited. For corporate buyers, it is the only legal document that allows them to claim credit for tax paid on business purchases. If any mandatory fields are missing or recorded incorrectly, the invoice becomes invalid, preventing your clients from claiming credits and potentially triggering audits or tax penalties.

In this guide, we examine the **mandatory GST invoice fields** required under GST Rule 46. Whether you are setting up templates or using our Free Invoice Builder, use this checklist to keep your billing fully compliant.

2. The 16 Mandatory Fields of GST Rule 46

GST Rule 46 specifies 16 key data points that must appear on every tax invoice. Missing any of these details can make the invoice invalid for tax credit purposes:

  1. Name, address, and GSTIN of the supplier (seller).
  2. A consecutive, unique serial number sequence, unique for each financial year, not exceeding 16 characters.
  3. Date of issue.
  4. Name, address, and GSTIN of the recipient (buyer) if registered.
  5. Name and address of the recipient, address of delivery, and the state name and code (mandatory for unregistered buyers if transaction value exceeds ₹50,000).
  6. HSN code for goods or SAC code for services.
  7. Description of goods or services.
  8. Quantity and unit of measurement.
  9. Total value of supply of goods or services.
  10. Taxable value of supply, taking into account discount amounts.
  11. Rate of tax (CGST, SGST, IGST, or Cess).
  12. Total tax amount charged.
  13. Place of supply, along with the state name and code (for interstate transactions).
  14. Address of delivery where it is different from the place of supply.
  15. Whether tax is payable on reverse charge basis.
  16. Signature or digital signature of the supplier or authorized representative.

3. Supplier Details (Seller Identity)

The supplier details form the identity anchor of the invoice. It must display your legal trading name, corporate office address, and active 15-digit GSTIN clearly. If your business operates multiple branches, the invoice must list the address of the specific branch making the supply.

4. Recipient Details (Buyer Identity)

If the recipient is registered under GST, you must list their legal business name, billing address, and 15-digit GSTIN. Recording the correct buyer GSTIN is critical; typos will prevent the transaction from appearing in the client's GSTR-2B dashboard, preventing their tax credit claims. For unregistered buyers, if the transaction value is less than ₹50,000, recording their GSTIN is not required. However, if the value exceeds ₹50,000, you must record the buyer's name, billing address, delivery location, and state code to comply with Place of Supply rules.

5. Transaction Meta (Dates & Sequence Numbers)

Every invoice must feature a unique, consecutive serial number sequence unique for each financial year. The sequence can contain alphanumeric characters, hyphens, and slashes. Proper serial sequencing is essential for tax audits, GSTR-1 filings, and tracking business transactions. The date of issue must also be clearly listed.

6. Item Tables, HSN Codes, and Tax Calculations

The body table of a tax invoice must provide a clear breakdown of each item or service:

  • Descriptions: Clear names and specifications of products or services.
  • HSN/SAC Codes: Classification codes (typically 4 or 6 digits) identifying the items under GST rules. You can find correct codes using our HSN Finder.
  • Quantities & Rates: Quantities, units of measurement, unit prices, and subtotals.
  • GST splits: Calculations displaying the base subtotal, tax rates, tax splits (CGST + SGST for intra-state or IGST for inter-state), and grand gross total. Check tax calculations using our GST Calculator.

7. Exemptions and Variations (Bill of Supply & Unregistered Buyers)

Not every transaction requires a standard tax invoice. Key variations include:

Bill of Supply: Issued when selling tax-exempt goods or services, or by registered composition dealers who are not allowed to charge GST to customers. It does not display tax rates or CGST/SGST/IGST calculations.

Proforma Invoice: A preliminary agreement showing terms of sale, issued before work begins or goods are shipped, often to secure advance payment. It is not a legal tax invoice and does not support tax credit claims. Learn more on our Proforma Invoice page.

8. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

What is the limit for characters in a GST invoice number?

Under GST rules, an invoice serial number must be unique and cannot exceed 16 characters in length, including numbers, letters, hyphens, and slashes.

Is HSN code mandatory for all service bills?

Yes. Service providers must list the correct 6-digit SAC (Services Accounting Code) on all invoices regardless of turnover to remain compliant.

Can I edit a GST invoice after issuing it?

No. Once an invoice has been issued or uploaded to the GST portal, you cannot modify it directly. Instead, you must issue a Credit Note or a Debit Note to make legal adjustments to the transaction value.
