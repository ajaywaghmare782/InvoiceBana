Common GST Invoice Mistakes & How to Avoid Them

Published on January 2025 • 8 min read

Introduction

Even small mistakes on GST invoices can lead to rejection, mismatch notices, and penalties. Here are the top 10 mistakes businesses make and how to avoid them.

Mistake #1: Missing or Incorrect GSTIN

Problem: The supplier's GSTIN is missing or wrong on the invoice.

Impact: Invoice becomes invalid. Buyer cannot claim input credit. GSTR-1 filing becomes complicated.

Solution: Always verify your GSTIN before issuing invoices. Use invoicing software to auto-fill it.

Mistake #2: Wrong Buyer GSTIN

Problem: Buyer's GSTIN is incorrect or mistyped.

Impact: Creates mismatch between your GSTR-1 and buyer's GSTR-2A. Can trigger demand notices.

Solution: Verify buyer's GSTIN at the time of sale. Check official GSTIN registry if unsure.

Mistake #3: Missing HSN/SAC Codes

Problem: Products/services listed without HSN/SAC codes.

Impact: Non-compliant with Rule 46. Penalty of ₹100 per item up to ₹5 lakh.

Solution: Use HSN codes for all goods and SAC codes for all services. Use the HSN Finder tool to get correct codes.

Mistake #4: Incorrect Tax Calculation

Problem: CGST, SGST, or IGST calculated wrong due to rounding or human error.

Impact: Customer overpays or underpays. Tax discrepancy in returns. Interest and penalties.

Solution: Use invoicing software that auto-calculates taxes. Verify calculations manually before sending invoices.

Mistake #5: Wrong Tax Type (CGST/SGST vs IGST)

Problem: Using CGST+SGST for inter-state sales or IGST for intra-state sales.

Impact: Invoice non-compliant. Buyer cannot claim correct input credit. Creates GSTR-1 issues.

Solution: Check buyer's state. Same state = CGST+SGST. Different state = IGST. Use tools that auto-detect.

Mistake #6: Missing Invoice Number or Date

Problem: Invoice doesn't have a number or date or has duplicates.

Impact: Cannot track invoices. GSTR-1 filing becomes impossible. Audit risk.

Solution: Maintain sequential invoice numbering. Always include date. Never reuse invoice numbers.

Mistake #7: Not Reporting Amendments/Credit Notes

Problem: Goods returned or discounts given but credit notes not issued or reported.

Impact: Overstates revenue and GST collected. Mismatch with buyer's records. Audit flags.

Solution: Issue credit notes immediately when returns occur. Report in GSTR-1. Maintain copies for 6 years.

Mistake #8: Incomplete Buyer Details

Problem: Buyer's name, address, or GSTIN missing or incomplete.

Impact: Invoice not fully compliant. Buyer cannot process invoice. Creates discrepancies.

Solution: Collect and verify complete buyer information before issuing invoice. Use standardized address format.

Mistake #9: Incorrect Amount in Words

Problem: Grand total amount written in words doesn't match the amount in figures.

Impact: Creates confusion. Professional image damaged. Dispute if amount is ambiguous.

Solution: Use invoicing software that auto-converts amounts to words. Verify manually before sending.

Mistake #10: Late Invoice Issuance

Problem: Invoice issued 30+ days after supply of goods/services.

Impact: Non-compliant with Rule 46 timeline. Cannot be claimed as valid supply in buyer's GSTR-2A.

Solution: Issue invoices immediately on or before delivery. Use automated invoicing systems to ensure timely issuance.

Additional Common Mistakes

Mistake #11: Wrong Rate of Tax

Using 18% tax rate when it should be 12%. Impact: Can trigger demand notices with interest.

Mistake #12: Mixing Different Items on One Invoice

Mixing taxable and exempt goods without clear separation can confuse tax calculation.

Mistake #13: Discount Not Reflected

Offering discount but not reducing taxable value. Tax charged on full amount even though customer paid less.

Mistake #14: No Terms and Conditions

While not mandatory, missing T&C can lead to disputes about payment terms and delivery.

Checklist Before Sending Invoice

Before issuing any invoice, verify:

  • ☐ Invoice has unique, sequential number
  • ☐ Invoice has a date (not in future)
  • ☐ Your GSTIN is correct and present
  • ☐ Buyer's GSTIN is correct (if B2B)
  • ☐ Buyer's name and address are complete
  • ☐ HSN/SAC codes are present for all items
  • ☐ Tax rate is correct for each item
  • ☐ GST calculations are accurate
  • ☐ Amount in words matches amount in figures
  • ☐ Terms and conditions are included
  • ☐ Invoice is issued on time (before/on delivery)
  • ☐ Discounts are reflected in taxable value
