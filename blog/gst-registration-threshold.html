GST Registration Threshold 2025: ₹20L and ₹40L Limits

Published on January 2025 • 8 min read

Introduction

One of the most important questions for new Indian businesses is: "Do I need to register for GST?" The answer depends on your annual turnover and whether it crosses the GST registration threshold. Let's understand the 2025 limits.

The ₹20 Lakh Threshold

Basic Rule:

If your annual turnover is ₹20 Lakh or more, you must register for GST.

Turnover Definition:

  • Includes all receipts from business activities
  • Calculated in a financial year (April-March)
  • For new businesses, annualized if started mid-year
  • Includes both taxable and exempt supplies

Who Must Register Under ₹20L?

  • Online traders/e-commerce: Mandatory at any turnover (post-2021)
  • Most service providers: If turnover ≥ ₹20L
  • Goods traders: If turnover ≥ ₹20L
  • Manufacturers: If turnover ≥ ₹20L

The ₹40 Lakh Threshold (Special Cases)

Who Gets ₹40L Threshold?

Certain businesses in Northeastern states and Himachal Pradesh get a higher threshold of ₹40L:

  • Arunachal Pradesh
  • Assam
  • Manipur
  • Meghalaya
  • Mizoram
  • Nagaland
  • Sikkim
  • Tripura
  • Himachal Pradesh

Important: Only businesses operating in these states get the ₹40L limit. If you operate across states, the higher threshold may not apply.

Who is Exempt From GST Registration?

1. Small Turnover

Annual turnover less than ₹20L (₹40L in special states) can operate without GST registration.

2. Exempt Suppliers

Businesses making only exempt supplies (like education, healthcare) don't need registration.

3. Registered Governments

Government departments don't register for GST.

4. Non-Taxable Persons

Some entities don't fit into the taxable person category.

Voluntary Registration

Key Point: Even if your turnover is below ₹20L, you can voluntarily register for GST.

Why Voluntarily Register?

  • Claim input tax credit on your purchases
  • Supply to GST-registered customers (B2B)
  • Improve business credibility
  • Expand market reach (some customers only buy from registered businesses)
  • E-commerce requirements

What Happens When You Cross ₹20L?

Timeline for Registration

When your turnover reaches ₹20L, you must register within 30 days of crossing the threshold.

From Which Date Do You Pay GST?

GST is payable from the date you cross ₹20L, not from registration date. So if you cross ₹20L on May 1, you owe GST from May 1 onwards.

Consequences of Late Registration

  • Can be asked to pay GST retroactively with interest
  • Penalties up to ₹25,000
  • Business reputation damage
  • Customers may reject invoices

How to Calculate Your Turnover

Include:

  • All sales revenue
  • Service income
  • Professional fees
  • Rental income from business property
  • Advances received
  • Stock transfers between states

Exclude:

  • Return of goods (if handled via credit note)
  • Discounts (if granted before invoice)
  • Personal loans or advances
  • Investment income
  • Insurance proceeds

Registration Process (Quick Steps)

  1. Visit GST Portal
  2. Click "Registration" → "New Registration"
  3. Fill Form GST REG-01 with your details
  4. Submit and get reference number
  5. Verification by tax officer (if needed)
  6. Receive GSTIN (usually 3-7 days)

Documents Required for Registration

  • PAN of business owner
  • Business address proof (electricity bill, rent agreement, etc.)
  • Bank account details
  • Mobile number and email
  • Previous 3-month bank statements
  • For partnership: Partnership deed
  • For company: Certificate of incorporation, board resolution

Quarterly Accounting Period Option

New in 2020: Businesses with turnover ≤ ₹5 crore can opt for quarterly GSTR returns (instead of monthly) if registered voluntarily or with turnover ≤ ₹1.5 crore.

Composition Scheme (₹1.5 Crore Limit)

Separate from the ₹20L threshold, there's a Composition Scheme for businesses with turnover ≤ ₹1.5 crore:

  • Pay fixed GST rate (1% for traders, 6% for service providers)
  • No GSTR-1 or GSTR-3B filing required
  • Cannot claim input tax credit
  • Cannot supply to other registered businesses
  • File quarterly return (GSTR-4) only

Multi-State Operations

If you operate in multiple states, calculate turnover as:

  • Total turnover from all states combined
  • If total ≥ ₹20L, you must register in the state where principal place of business is located
  • Can register in other states if you have supplies there

Checklist: Do You Need GST Registration?

  • ☐ Annual turnover ≥ ₹20L (or ₹40L in special states)?
  • ☐ Are you an online e-commerce seller/courier?
  • ☐ Do you need to supply to GST-registered businesses?
  • ☐ Will your customers ask for invoices from registered business?
  • ☐ Is your business across state lines?

If yes to any above: You need GST registration.

Resources

  • Official GST Portal: https://www.gst.gov.in
  • CBIC Guidelines: https://www.cbic.gov.in
  • Consult a CA for customized advice

Conclusion

The ₹20L threshold is the key point for GST registration. While the limit is generous for small businesses, it's important to stay on top of your turnover and register on time if you cross it. 💡 Tip: Even if you're below ₹20L, registering for GST can help your business grow by allowing you to claim input credit and supply to larger businesses!
