Freelancer Invoicing Guide: How to Bill Clients in India

1. Invoicing Landscape for Indian Freelancers

The gig economy in India is expanding rapidly, with millions of software developers, content writers, designers, marketing consultants, and tutors working as independent freelancers. While freelancing offers flexibility, it also requires managing your own business operations, particularly invoicing and tax compliance. An invoice is not just a request for payment; it is a legal document that records your income and provides your client with the documentation they need for their own accounts.

In this guide, we examine the compliance rules, tax thresholds, and layout formats required for Indian freelancers. Using a professional **invoice maker** like InvoiceBana can help you keep your billing compliant, secure, and professional.

2. GST Registration Thresholds for Services

Understanding when you need to register under GST is critical:

Turnover Threshold: For service providers in India, GST registration is mandatory if your annual aggregate turnover exceeds ₹20 lakhs (or ₹10 lakhs for northeastern and hill states). Aggregate turnover includes all taxable, exempt, and export sales.

Inter-state supply exception: Previously, any interstate supply required mandatory GST registration. Under current rules, service providers with aggregate turnover below ₹20 lakhs are exempt from GST registration even if they provide services to clients in other states. If you are unregistered, issue a standard commercial invoice without charging taxes.

3. Selecting the Correct SAC Classification Codes

GST uses SAC (Services Accounting Code) to classify services. Registered freelancers must list the correct 6-digit SAC code on their invoices. Common codes for freelancers include:

  • 998313: Information technology (IT) design and development services.
  • 998314: Web hosting, portal management, and domain services.
  • 998381: Photographic and videography services.
  • 998391: Copywriting, translation, and editorial services.
  • 998311: Management consulting and business advisory services.

4. How to Draft a GST Tax Invoice

If you are registered under GST, you must issue a tax invoice to domestic clients. Group your services in a clear line-items table, list the correct SAC code, and select the appropriate GST rate (typically 18% for professional services). If the client is in the same state, split the tax into CGST (9%) and SGST (9%). If the client is in a different state, apply IGST (18%) in full. 5. Invoicing Foreign Clients and Export Rules (LUT)

For freelancers billing clients outside of India, the transaction is classified as an export of services. Under GST rules, exports are treated as "zero-rated supplies." You have two options for billing foreign clients:

Option A (Export under LUT): You can file a Letter of Undertaking (LUT) on the GST portal at the start of the financial year. This allows you to bill foreign clients without charging or paying GST. This is the preferred method for most freelancers as it avoids locking up cash flow in tax refunds.

Option B (Export with Tax Payment): You pay the 18% IGST on the export invoice and later file a claim with the tax department for a refund. This process can be slow and requires extra paperwork.

In both cases, your invoice should clearly state "Export of Services under LUT without payment of IGST" or "Export of Services on payment of IGST." Ensure you receive payments in foreign currency through approved banking channels within the mandated timelines.

6. Billing Best Practices to Ensure Quick Clearings

Keep your invoicing organized to ensure prompt payments:

  • Agree on Terms Early: Put payment terms (e.g., Net 15 days, 50% advance) in your contract before beginning work. Use proforma invoices to request advance payments.
  • Provide Bank Details Clearly: List your account number, IFSC code, and UPI ID clearly in the notes section of the invoice.
  • Deliver Clean PDFs: Send invoices in standard A4 PDF format. PDF format is readable on all devices and prevents accidental edits.
  • Follow Up Promptly: Send a polite email reminder a few days before the due date, and follow up immediately if a payment is late.

7. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Do freelancers need to register for GST to bill foreign clients?

If your aggregate turnover (including foreign sales) is below ₹20 lakhs, you are exempt from GST registration. However, if your turnover exceeds the threshold, you must register and can use an LUT to bill foreign clients at 0% tax.

What is a Letter of Undertaking (LUT)?

An LUT is a online declaration filed by exporters on the GST portal. It allows you to export goods or services without paying IGST, simplifying compliance and cash flow.
