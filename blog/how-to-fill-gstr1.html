How to Fill GSTR-1: Complete Step-by-Step Guide

Published on January 2025 • 8 min read

What is GSTR-1?

GSTR-1 is the Return of Outward Supplies (goods and services sold). Every registered GST business must file GSTR-1 monthly to report all supplies they've made. It's one of the most important compliance documents under GST.

Who Must File GSTR-1?

  • All GST-registered businesses
  • Except composition scheme members (except certain suppliers)
  • Except non-GST registered businesses
  • Due date: 11th of next month (or 13th with late fee)

Key Things to Report in GSTR-1

B2B (Business to Business) Supplies

Supplies to registered businesses where buyer's GSTIN is provided.

B2C (Business to Consumer) Supplies

Supplies to unregistered consumers or where GSTIN is not available.

Exempt Supplies

Goods/services that are not taxable under GST.

Exports

Supplies made outside India, often with 0% tax or exempt.

Credit Notes/Debit Notes

Adjustments due to returns or corrections.

Step-by-Step Filing Process

Step 1: Login to GST Portal

  • Go to https://www.gst.gov.in
  • Click "Services" → "Returns"
  • Enter your GSTIN and password

Step 2: Fill Invoice Details

  • Invoice number, date, and amount
  • Buyer's GSTIN (if B2B)
  • Invoice value and GST amount
  • HSN/SAC code

Step 3: Categorize Supplies

Classify each supply as:

  • B2B (Intra-state or Inter-state)
  • B2C (Intra-state or Inter-state)
  • Exempt
  • Nil-rated/0%
  • Export

Step 4: Fill Tax Details

  • CGST amount
  • SGST amount
  • IGST amount
  • Cess (if applicable)

Step 5: Report Amendments

  • Credit notes issued
  • Debit notes issued
  • Reason for adjustment

Step 6: Review and Submit

  • Verify all data
  • Check totals match your accounting records
  • Submit electronically with digital signature

Example GSTR-1 Entry

Scenario: You sold goods worth ₹10,000 (with 18% GST) to a registered buyer in Delhi. Your GSTIN is in Delhi too.

  • Invoice Number: INV001
  • Invoice Date: 01-01-2025
  • Buyer GSTIN: 07AABCU9205D1Z0
  • Taxable Value: ₹10,000
  • CGST (9%): ₹900
  • SGST (9%): ₹900
  • Total GST: ₹1,800
  • Invoice Value: ₹11,800

Common GSTR-1 Mistakes to Avoid

  • Entering wrong buyer GSTIN (causes mismatch with buyer's GSTR-2A)
  • Forgetting to report B2C supplies
  • Incorrect HSN/SAC codes
  • Wrong tax categories (mixing CGST/SGST with IGST)
  • Not reporting amendments/credit notes
  • Late filing (missing the 11th/13th deadline)
  • Rounding errors in tax amounts

Reconciliation with Your Books

Before submitting GSTR-1, ensure:

  • Total invoices in GSTR-1 match your Sales Register
  • Total GST matches your GST collected
  • All amendments and credit notes are reported
  • No duplicate entries
  • All invoices have unique numbers

What Happens After Filing GSTR-1?

GSTR-2A Auto-Population

Your buyer receives a GSTR-2A automatically, showing what you've reported. The buyer uses this to claim input credit.

GSTR-3B Filing

By 20th of next month, you file GSTR-3B, which reconciles your outward supplies (GSTR-1) with inward supplies (GSTR-2A) and calculates net GST payable.

Tips for Easy GSTR-1 Compliance

  • Use invoicing software that auto-generates GSTR-1
  • Maintain invoices in proper order by date
  • Record GSTIN of all buyers at the time of sale
  • Keep original invoices for 6 years
  • Review GSTR-2A monthly for discrepancies
  • File on time to avoid penalties

Penalties for Incorrect GSTR-1

  • Late filing: ₹100-500 per day
  • Incorrect data: GST amount to be paid with interest
  • Mismatch with GSTR-2A: Demand notice may be issued

Conclusion

GSTR-1 is crucial for GST compliance and avoiding penalties. By maintaining proper invoices with all mandatory details (done easily with InvoiceBana) and filing on time, you can make GSTR-1 filing a breeze. If confused, consult a chartered accountant.
