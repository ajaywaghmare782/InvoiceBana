How to Create a GST Invoice: Step-by-Step Billing Guide

1. Introduction to GST Invoicing

Under the Goods and Services Tax (GST) laws in India, issuing a legally binding tax invoice is a core compliance requirement for registered taxpayers. An invoice acts as the legal record of supply, recording the value of goods or services provided, terms of trade, and taxes collected. For corporate buyers, a correct GST invoice is the only way to claim Input Tax Credit (ITC). If your invoices contain errors or miss key details, your clients will not be able to claim their credits, causing billing disputes and delays.

In this comprehensive guide, we walk you through the step-by-step process of creating a GST invoice. Whether you run a retail shop, operate a consulting agency, or work as an independent freelancer, this guide will help you keep your billing compliant, secure, and professional.

2. Pre-requisites for Generating GST Invoices

Before you draw up your first GST invoice, make sure you have the following information and credentials ready:

  • GSTIN (GST Identification Number): A unique 15-digit registration code issued by the tax department after registration. If your annual turnover exceeds ₹40 lakhs for goods (or ₹20 lakhs for services), registration is mandatory.
  • Unique Serial Numbers: A consecutive, sequential numbering sequence containing letters, numbers, and symbols, unique for each financial year.
  • HSN/SAC Codes: Classification codes identifying physical goods (HSN) or professional services (SAC) to determine the correct GST tax rates. You can find these using our HSN Finder tool.
  • Client GST Details: The legal name, billing address, and 15-digit GSTIN of the buyer.

4. Mandatory Fields Checklist (GST Rule 46)

The Central Board of Indirect Taxes and Customs (CBIC) outlines strict rules for invoice formatting. Under GST Rule 46, a tax invoice must contain the following details:

  • Name, address, and GSTIN of the supplier (seller).
  • A consecutive, unique serial number sequence, not exceeding 16 characters.
  • Date of its issue and date of supply.
  • Name, address, and GSTIN of the recipient (buyer) if registered.
  • HSN code for goods or SAC code for services.
  • Description of goods or services.
  • Quantity and unit of measurement.
  • Total value of supply, discount amounts, and taxable value.
  • Rate of tax (CGST, SGST, IGST) and total tax amount.
  • Place of supply, along with the state name and code.
  • Whether tax is payable on reverse charge basis.
  • Signature or digital signature of the supplier or authorized representative.

5. Calculating CGST, SGST, and IGST Splits

Determining which GST column to apply is based on the location of the seller (supplier) relative to the location of the buyer (recipient):

Intra-State Supply (CGST + SGST): If both parties are located in Maharashtra, the supply occurs within the same state. The total tax is split evenly between the Central Government and the State Government. For an 18% GST rate, you apply 9% Central GST (CGST) and 9% State GST (SGST).

Inter-State Supply (IGST): If the supplier is in Maharashtra and the buyer is in Karnataka, the supply is inter-state. The tax is collected in full by the Central Government as Integrated GST (IGST). For an 18% rate, the invoice will show a single 18% IGST line item. Use our GST Calculator to check these calculations quickly.

6. Role of HSN and SAC Classification Codes

HSN (Harmonized System of Nomenclature) and SAC (Services Accounting Code) classify goods and services under GST. For businesses with turnover above ₹5 crores, HSN codes are mandatory. Service providers use 6-digit SAC codes regardless of turnover. Using incorrect classification codes can lead to audits, tax rate discrepancies, and delays in ITC claims.

7. Common GST Billing Mistakes to Avoid

Avoid these common mistakes to keep your business billing running smoothly:

  • Incorrect State Selection: Selecting the wrong state for the supplier or buyer results in incorrect CGST/SGST/IGST tax splits, requiring you to cancel the invoice and issue a credit note.
  • Non-Sequential Serial Numbers: Running duplicate or randomized invoice numbers disrupts your bookkeeping and can trigger auditing reviews.
  • Missing HSN/SAC Codes: Leaving HSN/SAC columns empty can result in your client's accounts department rejecting the invoice.
  • Incorrect GSTIN: Typos in your client's 15-digit GSTIN will prevent them from receiving their Input Tax Credit on the GST portal.

8. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Can I write GST invoices without a GSTIN?

If your annual turnover is below the threshold and you are not registered under GST, you cannot charge GST or issue a tax invoice. Instead, you should issue a commercial invoice or Bill of Supply without listing tax rates.

What happens if I make a mistake on a GST invoice?

Under GST rules, once an invoice is uploaded, you cannot modify it directly. Instead, you must issue a Credit Note (to reduce the value) or a Debit Note (to increase it) to adjust the transaction value legally.

Is an online GST invoice legally valid?

Yes. Digital invoices generated online are fully valid under GST laws as long as they contain all mandatory data points required by GST Rule 46.
