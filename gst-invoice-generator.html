What is a GST Invoice Generator?

A GST invoice generator is a technical web application that helps businesses, retailers, and service providers calculate, compile, and format invoices in accordance with India's Goods and Services Tax (GST) laws. Under the GST framework introduced in 2017, all registered businesses must provide customers with legally compliant invoices. An online tax bill generator automates the tedious arithmetic, splits rates correctly into intra-state or inter-state columns, and structures data according to CBIC guidelines, ensuring business compliance without manual errors.

For independent freelancers, sole proprietorships, partnerships, and growing startups, maintaining standard compliance is crucial. A valid tax invoice allows corporate buyers to claim Input Tax Credit (ITC). If your document lacks required fields, such as your GSTIN, the correct HSN/SAC classification, or correct CGST/SGST/IGST tax splits, your client cannot claim their tax credits, which can lead to payment disputes and damage professional relationships.

GST Tax Invoice Requirements (GST Rule 46)

The Central Board of Indirect Taxes and Customs (CBIC) outlines strict guidelines for issuing tax invoices. Under GST Rule 46, a tax invoice must contain the following details:

  • Supplier Information: Name, physical address, and 15-digit GSTIN (GST Identification Number).
  • Recipient Details: Name, billing address, and GSTIN of the client (if registered).
  • State of Supply: Essential for determining whether to charge CGST+SGST or IGST.
  • Invoice Serial Number: A consecutive, unique numbering sequence containing letters, numbers, and hyphens, unique for each financial year.
  • Invoice Date: Date of issue and date of supply of goods or services.
  • Description of Supply: Specific details of the items or services being billed.
  • HSN/SAC Codes: Harmonized System of Nomenclature for goods, or Services Accounting Codes.
  • Quantity & Units: Clear units of measurements (e.g., kilograms, hours, pieces).
  • Value Breakdown: Subtotal before tax, applicable GST percentage (e.g., 5%, 12%, 18%, 28%), calculated tax splits, and total gross value.
  • Declaration & Signature: A standard disclaimer declaring that details are true and correct.

Understanding CGST, SGST, and IGST Tax Splits

The GST framework features three tax columns. Determining which column to apply is based on the location of the seller (supplier) relative to the location of the buyer (recipient):

Intra-State Supply (CGST + SGST): If your business is registered in Maharashtra and you bill a buyer located in Mumbai, Maharashtra, the supply occurs within the same state. Here, the total tax is split evenly between the Central Government and the State Government. If the rate is 18%, you apply 9% Central GST (CGST) and 9% State GST (SGST).

Inter-State Supply (IGST): If your Maharashtra-based business bills a client situated in Karnataka, the supply is inter-state. The tax is collected in full by the Central Government as Integrated GST (IGST). For an 18% rate, the invoice will show a single 18% IGST line item. Our online GST bill generator calculates these splits automatically based on the states you select for the supplier and buyer.

HSN and SAC Code Requirements

To ensure proper categorization of transactions, CBIC mandates listing HSN (for physical goods) and SAC (for digital or professional services) codes. For small businesses with an annual turnover up to ₹5 crores, HSN listing requires a 4-digit code. For entities exceeding ₹5 crores, a 6-digit code is required. Service providers use 6-digit SAC codes regardless of turnover. Using our HSN Finder helps you identify correct codes quickly, ensuring your invoices remain legally valid.

Legal Validity of Digital Invoices

Under Indian commercial law, a digital GST invoice generated online is fully valid and legally binding, provided it includes all mandatory fields required by GST Rule 46. Once generated, download the PDF, print or email it directly, and archive a copy to simplify filing your monthly GSTR-1 and GSTR-3B tax returns.
